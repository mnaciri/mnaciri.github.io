History of dog and human relation

When friendship crosses borders between species

The relation between human and dog has existed 40,000 years ago. It started when humanity began to hunt. These thousands years relation explain why today dogs can be very attached to humans.

The origin of the dog started from the divergence with wolves. Scientists agree that 15,000 to 40,000 years ago, humans started to breed dogs. They wanted them to fulfill a need of protection and hunting teammate.

Scientific evidence supposes that the first dogs were grey wolves. And after several years of selected breeding, we have a lot of breeds' dog in the world. At the beginning, wolves where selected for their obedience and their hunt ability. The use of dogs in our society has changed many times. That's why we have a lot of varieties of dogs. Some breeders tried to change the appearance or the performances of their dog.

Human and dog friendship in literature

Romulus and Remus were the founders of Roma, Italy in Roman mythology. Children of the god of Mars, they were abandoned and found by a wolf who suckled them.

 The she-wolf feeding Romulus and Remus

Lassie is a book written by Eric Knight in 1940. It tells the story of Lassie the dog which is very intelligent and owned by Joe Carraclough. Unfortunatly, Joe's father in Britain, has to sell Lassie to earn money. Lassie has been brought in Scotland by the new owner but she decided to escape and go back to reach Joe. After dangers and aventures, she finally came back to the Carraclough family.

White fang is a book written by Jack London in 1906. The story starts by the born of white fang, a dog wolf from a half-dog half-wolf mother and a wolf father. After a short life with wolves, he discorvers life with humans. His owner decided to turn him into a fighting dog. During a fight with a stronger dog, two men stopped the fight to save White fang. With patience and love, a friendship started between him and the 2 men.
